\textbf{Context:} The growing adoption of Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems has intensified concerns regarding transparency, fairness, and accountability, especially in light of documented incidents of algorithmic bias, discriminatory automated decisions, and limited auditability. Although ethical guidelines and regulatory frameworks (e.g., UNESCO, OECD, EU AI Act) provide high-level principles, the literature reveals a lack of technical mechanisms to operationalize them throughout the Software Engineering lifecycle, particularly during the \textit{Testing} phase. \textbf{Objective:} This dissertation aims to develop and evaluate a set of automated \textit{fuzzing engines} capable of detecting ethical risks related to \textit{Transparency}, \textit{Fairness}, and \textit{Accountability} in deployed AI models, providing tools that can be integrated into real-world Software Engineering workflows. \textbf{Method:} The research follows a \textit{Design Science Research} (DSR) approach. A Systematic Mapping Study (2020--2025) was conducted to identify the most prevalent ethical principles and existing technical gaps. Ethical risks were then specified, mapped to suitable fuzzing techniques, and implemented in three modular \textit{fuzzing engines}. Finally, these engines will be empirically evaluated using fuzzing campaigns executed on commercial and open-source AI models. \textbf{Results:} The mapping revealed \textit{Fairness}, \textit{Accountability}, and \textit{Transparency} as the most prominent principles in the literature and also those with the most concrete operationalization approaches. Key gaps include the lack of automated, integrable tools and limited coverage of ethical testing. The implemented engines provide automated test-case generation, principle specific oracles, and metrics for ethical compliance. \textbf{Conclusion:} The proposed artifacts are expected to reduce the gap between abstract ethical principles and practical verification mechanisms, enabling systematic detection of ethical failures in AI models and contributing to more robust governance, quality assurance, and auditing practices within Software Engineering. Moreover, this work advances the state of the art by offering a modular and automated approach for ethical testing, directly addressing gaps identified in the mapping study.
