\textbf{Context:} In recent years, the field of artificial intelligence has undergone a remarkable process of expansion, both at the academic and industrial levels. This growth can be observed in various ways, including the development of more complex technologies, increased investment, greater media attention and the expansion of its areas of application. However, this advancement has also given rise to a number of ethical issues that are becoming a matter of growing social concern, including system bias and the potential for harmful applications.
\textbf{Objective:}  
This work aims to present an overview of the current state of practical solutions for the ethical development of AI-based systems at all stages of a software system's life cycle. Additionally, it seeks to develop a tool that operationalises the translation of high-level ethical requirements into ethical user stories.
\textbf{Methods:} 
In order to achieve this objective, this study was based on the Design Science Research methodology, which has thus far yielded three results. Initially, in the problem awareness phase, the systematic literature review developed by Cerqueira \cite{cerqueirahiccs} was updated. Subsequently, in the suggestion phase, the proposed tool was formulated as a system that translates ethical requirements into ethical user stories. Finally, in the development phase, the first version of the tool was implemented.
\textbf{Results:} A total of 38 primary studies were identified. The majority of these studies (63\%) put forward a practical solution to facilitate the application of ethics in AI. However, there remains a gap between theory and practice when it comes to ethics in AI. Furthermore, a list of 26 of the most frequently discussed ethical principles in the literature was compiled. Following this, the US Translator tool was proposed and developed. This tool uses user stories, AI ethics principles and LLM models to facilitate the integration of AI ethics during the requirements engineering phase.
\textbf{Conclusion:} The outcomes of the model's training process demonstrate its potential as a valuable tool for the early integration of AI ethics into software development. The preliminary results indicate that the tool supports the integration of theoretical and practical aspects, offering a promising solution to an existing gap in the current range of practical applications.
